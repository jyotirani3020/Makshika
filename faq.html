FAQs

How do bees make honey?

Honey is the sweet fluid produced by honeybees from the nectar of flowers. Worker honeybees transform the floral nectar that they gather into honey by adding enzymes to the nectar and reducing the moisture.

How is honey extracted from the hive honeycomb?

Hive is regularly inspected and as soon as the honeycomb in the hive is filled with honey and capped with beeswax it is ready to be harvested. The frames containing combs are removed from the hives and uncapped. They are put in centrifuges to remove the honey from the honeycombs. Honey is also sold with honeycombs also.

Does honey have an expiration date?

Honey is perhaps the only food that really never expires. Keep it for 10 years, 20 years, 50 years or more and it will still taste the same as it did when it was stored. Don't believe it? National Geographic reported that archaeologists found 3,000-year-old pots of perfectly edible honey when unearthing Egyptian tombs. This is due to honey’s hygroscopic and low pH, two qualities that make it difficult for the sticky substance to spoil.

What If My Honey Crystallizes?

Crystallization is a natural process affected by temperature, the ratio of glucose and fructose, and pollen. Crystallized honey is still delicious and safe to eat, just scoop out of the jar and spread it on your toast or drop it in your tea!

But if you’d rather go for the liquid form, Place the honey in a microwave-safe container with the lid off and microwave, stirring every 30 seconds, until the crystals dissolve Simply place the container in a bowl of warm water and stir. Be careful not to boil or scorch the honey.

What is raw honey?

Raw honey is collected directly from the honey comb and is not processed, heated, or pasteurized.

What is filtered honey?

Filtered honey is honey that has been filtered to the extent that all or most of the fine particles, pollen grains, air bubbles and other materials normally found in suspension have been removed.

All honey crystallizes eventually. Suspended particles and fine air bubbles in honey contribute to faster crystallization. Filtering helps delay crystallization, helping the honey to remain liquid for a much longer period than unfiltered honey.

Cooking with honey – How do I substitute honey for sugar?

When substituting honey for granulated sugar in recipes, begin by substituting honey for up to half of the sugar called for in the recipe. For baked goods, make sure to reduce the oven temperature by 25°F to prevent overbrowning; reduce any liquid called for by 1/4 cup for each cup of honey used and add 1/2 teaspoon baking soda for each cup of honey used. Honey can be measured easily by using the same cup used for measuring the oil in a recipe or by coating a cup or spoon with nonstick vegetable spray.

Because of its high fructose content, honey has higher sweetening power than sugar. This means you can use less honey than sugar to achieve the desired sweetness.

How to Check if Your Honey is Pure or Adulterated

Examining honey before purchase – The Government of India has implemented the National Programme for Organic Production (NPOP). The national programme involves the accreditation programme for Certification Bodies, standards for organic production, promotion of organic farming etc.

Conducting home tests: Take a teaspoon of the honey and put in a glass full of water. Fake or adulterated honey will dissolve in the water while pure honey which has a more dense texture will settle right at the bottom of the glass as lumps. The same is the case with blotting paper or a white cloth.

What is the best way to store your honey once the jar has been opened?

The honey can be stored at room temperature as refrigeration is not necessary. If you like your honey thicker, the fridge is your best bet.
