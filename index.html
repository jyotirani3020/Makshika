Something about honey

Honey is perhaps the only food that really never expires. Keep it for 10 years, 20 years, 50 years or more and it will still taste the same as it did when it was stored. Don't believe it? National Geographic reported that archaeologists found 3,000-year-old pots of perfectly edible honey when unearthing Egyptian tombs.

Honey comes in different flavors including the traditional wildflower, orange, mustard, lemon just to name a few. These flavors don’t come from additives. They are created based on the plants from which the bees draw nectar and pollen.

What makes honey different from just about every other food? For starters, it has a very low water content and does not allow microbial growth that requires high water content for growth. Combine it with honey's low pH, and we've got an environment where it is nearly impossible for bacteria to grow. Finally, add in honey's antibacterial properties and we have a product with an eternal shelf life. However, to give a shelf life to a food item, the general recommendation for the shelf-life of honey is two year but this can vary.

Natural honey has more than 300 constituents, but its main composition is sugar, primarily in the form of fructose and glucose. It contains many minerals and vitamins. Honey is a versatile sweetener so there are countless ways to use it.
